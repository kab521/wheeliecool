Kawasaki Ninja 300 Review


A lightweight beginner sportbike with smooth power delivery, approachable handling, yet still fun even after the basics are mastered.


BY: KATHRYN BAGER • 05/05/2026



Product Information

Brand: Kawasaki
Model: Ninja 300
Engine: 296cc parallel twin
Type: Sport motorcycle
Best For: Beginner riders and daily commuting


Ratings

  • Overall: ★★★★☆
  • Performance: ★★★★☆
  • Comfort: ★★★★☆
  • Fuel Economy: ★★★★★
  • Technology: ★★★☆☆
  • Value: ★★★★★

Pros

✔ Lightweight and easy to control
✔ Great fuel efficiency
✔ Affordable entry-level motorcycle
✔ Comfortable for commuting

Cons

✘ Limited power for experienced riders

My freshman year at the University of Pittsburgh, I remember seeing this bike parked outside my dorm when I was walking back from class. I watched the girl ride off with it, and I remember thinking that that was the coolest thing ever, since I always thought motorcycles were the stereotypical ugly Harley-Davidson cruisers. But I knew I wanted to learn more about it and eventually save up for one. I remembered the name, and as I looked it up, I found out that this bike is actually a sport bike which is meant for speed rather than comfort. It also is actually one of the most common recommendations for beginner riders as well. And I thought, "how lucky is that?" I thought I would share my casual research about the bike in this review and frame it in the way I learned about it.

The Kawasaki Ninja 300 is a lightweight sport bike that has a strong balance between performance, comfort, and beginner-friendly handling. Sometimes referred to as the EX300, it is a 296 cc Ninja series sport bike introduced by Kawasaki in 2012 for the 2013 model year. It is sold in Asia, Australia, Europe, and North America. When introduced, the Ninja 300R replaced the Ninja 250R in some markets.

It has a 296cc twin-cylinder engine that produces around 39 horsepower. CCs stand for cubic centimeters, which is the size of the engine's lungs essentially. The higher the CC, the larger the engine, and the more power it has (usually, since it also depends on how the engine is built and how heavy the bike is). The twin-cylinder is the engine's configuration, and this one is compact, lightweight, and can reach high speeds. The higher the horsepower means that it has better acceleration, higher top speed, and better performance. The bike is stable, which will help new riders build confidence, while still being fun for more experienced riders, especially when pushed at higher revs. Despite the sporty ergonomics, it can still be comfortable for daily commuting and long rides, since it has a relaxed seating position and good wind protection.

However, since it was introduced in 2013, it can have some outdated features such as a basic instrument cluster. That means the screens behind the steering wheel that display vital vehicle data like speed, fuel level, engine temperature, and warning lights are more basic. In addition, the braking performance could be stronger under aggressive riding. It can also be a bit expensive for what it offers today at around $5,649. That is why a lot of riders recommend buying your first bike second-hand for much cheaper, sometimes around $1500, which is also safer if you accidentally damage it.

Overall, despite the minor negatives, its reliability, smooth engine, and versatility make it a great choice for beginner riders looking for a dependable, easy-to-ride sport bike.
